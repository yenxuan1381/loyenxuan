Projects

Hangman (Python)

During my semester break, I followed the MIT OpenCourseWare syllabus on the module 6.0001 Introduction to Computer Science, and their assignments were very fun, in assignment 2, I built a hangman game to be played on the python console. The secret word is randomly generated from a file of word list. You begin with 6 lifes and lose 1 life for every wrong guesses. There is also 3 warnings to prevent any same guesses or non-alphabet attempts. It reveals the secret word if you fail to guess it, and congrats you if you successfully guessed the secret word.

Scrabble (Python)

In the next assignment, I get to build a game like scrabble, where you choose how many hands you want to play, and form a valid word from the letters in your current hand. * can be used as any alphabet. The validity of the word is accessed based on a file of wordlist. The score is then calculated based on the length of your word and the letters you didn't use.

The program will filter the news and alert the user if a particular news story matches the user's interests, such as news having a particular keyword in their title or description, or news that were reported in a particular time or news that matches both the condition.
